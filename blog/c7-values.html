One of the most brutal yet empowering phrases I have ever come across is The Serenity Prayer. "Grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change, courage to change the things I can, and the wisdom to know the difference." I tend to not give up on something until I've reached a breaking point. It's an interesting experience to come out the other end having concluded that the only option possible is to accept. It's bittersweet. On the one hand it's a relief to be freed from the burdon, but at the same time it's hard to sacrifice the hope. I think "serenity" is an ongoing process that, if handled rationally, becomes an incremental step towards personal development and wisdom.
