What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Wow. It's been a whilrwind this week. I've never seen HTML or CSS before in my life until now. At first, it seemed very user-friendly and straightforward. The challenge comes, however, in creating the dance between the two languages that enbles you to create cool and new websites to your heart's content. In handling spacing, it's very important that you utilize the CSS Box Model. This takes the content and handles it with grouping so that you're then able to organize them more effeciently. The order of the concepts is as follows, going from the inside out: First is the content, comprised of text and images. Next comes the padding, which ensures that the content is legible and visually protected from the surrounding colors and images. Then comes the actual border. Because of the padding, you're freed to make it as dark or contrasted to the content as you prefer. Padding ensures that the text won't be flush to the edge of the box. Lastly is the margin. The margin is the spacing outside of the element. This is seemingly empty space that prevents the content from being flushed with either the edge of the screen, other content, or overlapping.

**A common mistake that I ran into is that if the margins touch, they collapse, and the larger of the two is used, so be on top of the pixel numbers.
